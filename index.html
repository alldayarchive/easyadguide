The AdSense Approval Roadmap

Follow these 3 phases to ensure your site is ready for monetization.

Phase 01: Site Structural Setup

Before applying, your site must have a logical structure. Google's crawler needs to navigate easily. Use a clean, responsive theme (like Tailwind or a lightweight WordPress theme). Ensure you have essential pages: About Us, Contact Us, and Privacy Policy.

  • ✅ Mobile-friendly design
  • ✅ Fast loading speed (< 2.5s)
  • ✅ Clear navigation menu

Phase 02: High-Value Content Strategy

Content is the most critical factor. Aim for at least 15-20 original, high-quality articles. Each post should be over 1,000 words. Focus on solving problems for your readers rather than just personal stories. Use H1, H2, and H3 tags correctly for SEO.

  • ✅ No "under construction" pages
  • ✅ Original images with Alt text
  • ✅ Proper text formatting and grammar

7 Golden Tips for Fast Approval

1. Use Custom Domain

Always use a top-level domain (.com, .net, .org). Subdomains (like .blogspot.com) take much longer to review.

2. Single Niche Focus

Don't mix health with crypto. Stick to one topic so Google can easily categorize your expertise.

3. Remove "Coming Soon"

Any placeholder content will lead to an immediate "Valuable Inventory: No Content" rejection.

4. Search Console Integration

Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console. If your pages aren't indexed, Google won't approve them.

5. User Navigation

Make sure your menu works perfectly. Broken links are a major red flag for the AdSense team.

6. Avoid Copyright Issues

Never copy-paste. Use tools like Grammarly and plagiarism checkers before publishing.

Troubleshooting Rejections

Rejection: "Low Value Content"

Reason: The bot found your content generic or too short.

Solution: Write 5 extra articles, each 1,500+ words. Add unique data, tables, or personal insights that can't be found elsewhere on the web.

Rejection: "Site Behavior/Navigation"

Reason: Hard to find content or broken buttons.

Solution: Simplify your menu. Ensure the "AdSense Code" is correctly placed between <head> tags and not causing layout shifts.
